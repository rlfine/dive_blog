Case Study: Enhancing Deep Diver Training with Emergency Action Drills

Practicing for the Emergency That You Hope Never Happens

Background

Traditional Deep Diver courses focus on achieving depth and understanding narcosis and gas planning. However, serious divers recognize that mastering deep-water emergency skills is critical to true competence and safety at depth.

Challenge

Standard certification dives may not include proactive training for failures like buoyancy compensator (BC) ruptures or regulator malfunctions. Without structured rehearsal of these emergencies, divers may be unprepared for real-world incidents at 100 feet or deeper.

Solution: Integrated Emergency Drills

By working closely with a NAUI instructor, a diver can integrate emergency action drills directly into the Deep Diver training dives. This approach maintains all standard certification objectives, while layering on vital real-world skills.

Proposed Deep Water Emergency Drills

  • Buoyancy Compensator (BC) Failure Simulation: Practice maintaining buoyancy control without BC inflation and conducting a safe ascent.
  • First Stage Regulator Failure Simulation: React to free-flowing or non-functional regulators, switch to backups, and ascend with control.
  • Out-of-Air Situations: Conduct buddy-assisted ascents using alternate air sources from 100 feet.
  • Primary Mask Removal: Remove and replace the mask at depth to simulate dislodgement or flooding.
  • Controlled Emergency Swimming Ascent (CESA): Practice emergency ascents from ~30–40 feet.
  • Redundant Gas Source Deployment: Utilize pony bottles or bailouts if equipped to ensure self-rescue capability.

Implementation Notes

  • Emergency abort procedures must be agreed upon before drills.
  • Instructor and buddy should maintain close proximity during all exercises.
  • Redundant gas supplies and surface marker buoys (SMBs) should be used whenever possible.
  • Only conduct CESA from safe moderate depths (not from 100 feet).

Conclusion

Advanced divers should take ownership of their continuing education. By requesting custom emergency training during a Deep Diver course, divers build deeper confidence, enhance safety, and become true thinking divers — embodying the spirit of NAUI training philosophy.

Here is a proposal to consider. Discuss this with your NAUI instructor. Make sure you mention that you want to fulfill all of the requirements of any course certification and that, if necessary, you would be willing to pay for the extra time required to work through the set of drills you want to master.
