Why ODIGTML Has Multiple Meanings

Exploring the science and safety of scuba diving.

Introduction – Why ODIGTML Has Multiple Meanings

If you're a NAUI diver or especially a dive leader in training, you may have encountered the acronym ODIGTML in more than one setting—sometimes meaning one thing, and sometimes another. That’s not a mistake, and it’s not sloppy instruction. It’s actually a feature of how NAUI teaches context-driven dive planning.

The acronym ODIGTML is intentionally reused in two distinct frameworks:

  • Risk-Based Dive Planning – where ODIGTML serves as a checklist to identify and mitigate dive hazards
  • Dive Group Planning – where ODIGTML helps teams structure a safe, organized, collaborative dive

While most acronyms strive for a single unambiguous meaning, NAUI appears to have made a pedagogical choice to reuse ODIGTML across domains because the letters still represent **seven planning pillars**, regardless of context. Instead of trying to teach two completely different acronyms for different types of dives, this reuse reinforces the idea that dive planning—whether for a team or a technical challenge—should always involve structured thinking across multiple categories.

So rather than seeing ODIGTML's dual use as a flaw, think of it as a mental tool that adapts to your environment. Same structure, different lens. What follows are two fully explained versions of ODIGTML, each matched to its planning context.

ODIGTML (Risk-Based) – A Guide to Identify and Mitigate Dive Risks

O – Oxygen

Evaluate PPO₂ exposure, MOD for Nitrox, CNS % tracking, and oxygen toxicity risk (CNS and pulmonary).

D – Decompression

Review NDL status or deco obligation, ascent rates, stop profiles, and algorithm or dive computer settings.

I – Inert Gas Narcosis

Assess narcosis risk at planned depth. Consider diver experience, mental readiness, and use of trimix if needed.

G – Gas Management

Calculate RMV/SAC, gas needed for descent, task load, ascent, reserve. Include turn pressure and bailout gas.

T – Thermal

Evaluate exposure suit needs based on water temperature and duration. Monitor for hypothermia or overheating.

M – Mission

Define the primary task (e.g., lift bag use, photo survey). Consider complexity and whether dive is abortable.

L – Logistics

Surface support, DAN O₂ kits, emergency response plan, chamber access, communications, extra gas or food.

The risk-assessment interpretation of ODIGTML is introduced in NAUI Advanced and Master Scuba Diver courses particularly in modules that emphasize hazard awareness, gas planning, and complex exposure management.

This version frames ODIGTML as a checklist for evaluating the seven core risk categories on any dive:
  • Oxygen exposure,
  • Decompression planning,
  • Inert gas narcosis,
  • Gas availability,
  • Thermal protection,
  • Mission complexity, and
  • Logistical support.
ODIGTML (Dive Group Planning) – A Guide for Group Dive Planning

O – Objective

Define the purpose of the dive. Example: Navigation practice, search & recovery training, fun dive, etc.

D – Dive Site Description

Identify site name, access method, conditions (depth, temperature, visibility), and known hazards.

I – Individual & Team Roles

Assign tasks: dive leader, buddy teams, equipment carriers, timing/depth monitors, etc.

G – Gear Requirements

List required equipment: wetsuits/drysuits, tanks, slates, SMBs, compasses, special tools.

T – Time, Depth & Profile

Plan max depth, bottom time, safety stop, ascent rate, dive profile (square, multi-level), and turn pressures.

M – Method of Dive

Describe entry/exit strategy, compass headings, signal use, task sequence, emergency abort criteria.

L – Logistics

List surface support, O₂ kits, transportation, gas fills, dive window, meals, weather watch, and check-in protocol.

The ODIGTML group planning model is introduced in NAUI Leadership-level coursework, particularly in the NAUI Divemaster and Instructor Training programs. It provides a structured, seven-part planning framework designed to ensure dive teams address objective, site, personnel, gear, timing, methods, and logistics.

While first introduced in the entry level courses, the two versions or interpretations serve different pedagogical purposes. NAUI's intentional reuse of ODIGTML in leadership courses reinforces the habit of structured, multi-factor planning in both recreational and technical settings. It is expected that by the time divers get to leadership levels, they will see and appreciate the reuse and not be caught up in the confusion of multiple meanings and interpretations.
