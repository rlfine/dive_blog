Foundational Scuba Diving Knowledge

As divers transition from rental equipment to their own gear, a solid foundation in dive physics, physiology, and marine awareness ensures informed decisions and safer dives.

If a diver is neutrally buoyant with 8 lbs of lead in fresh water, they typically need 2–4 additional pounds in salt water due to the increased density. For most divers, this means 10–12 lbs total. The exact amount depends on factors such as wetsuit thickness, tank type, and personal buoyancy.

Venomous creatures inject toxins via sting, bite, or puncture—like lionfish and cone snails. Poisonous animals are harmful if touched or ingested—like certain pufferfish organs. This distinction affects emergency responses and safety precautions.

Determine the object's weight in water by subtracting displaced water weight from dry weight. Since each liter of displacement provides ~2.2 lbs of lift, a 100 lb object displacing 30 liters would weigh 34 lbs underwater, requiring a lift bag rated for at least 40–45 lbs with margin.
