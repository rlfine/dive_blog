Dalton‚Äôs Law is the fundamental gas law in scuba diving used in decompression algorithms when computing theoretical nitrogen loading in tissues, but it is also used when performing partial pressure blending. The law states:

"The total pressure of a gas mixture is equal to the sum of the partial pressures of each individual gas in the mixture."

Formula:
Ptotal = PO‚ÇÇ + PN‚ÇÇ + ...

The converse of Dalton's Law is also true:

"The partial pressure of a gas in a mixture is equal to the fraction (or percentage) of that gas times the total pressure of the gas mixture."

It is the converse of Dalton's Law that we use in partial pressure blending.

In scuba blending, if you want to create a specific Nitrox mix (e.g., EAN36 at 3400 psi), you calculate how much of each gas (pure O‚ÇÇ and air) to add so their partial pressures sum to the desired total:

PO‚ÇÇ = 0.36 √ó 3400 = 1224 psi
PN‚ÇÇ = 3400 ‚àí 1224 = 2176 psi

This law enables precise gas blending for safe and optimized dive profiles.
